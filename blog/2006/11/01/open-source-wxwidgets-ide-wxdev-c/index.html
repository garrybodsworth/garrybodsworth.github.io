Open-Source wxWidgets IDE - wxDevC++

Nov 1st, 2006
In a previous post I have mentioned the splendid free IDE Code::Blocks, but in the dark old days of a few years ago, before Microsoft gave stuff away for free and development was quite an expensive endevour, there existed DevC++. It was a free IDE written in Delphi that provided good library management and also was an IDE for GCC in Windows.

Over the years other IDEs have sprung up and essentially overtaken Dev-C++ in terms of features and quality. Then someone decided to make a branch, one that was going to have integrated GUI editing. This project eventually became wxDevC++.

A new version has just been released with even more features from wxWidgets integrated into the GUI editor. Most importantly it now has support for Visual C++ compilers (v6, 2003, and 2005) which is interesting. I need to go and check out this IDE in more detail, but it is good to see it is more than alive and kicking. If you are a wxWidgets developer it is one of the most comprehensive integrated IDEs for developing and looks like it will be going from strength to strength judging from the changelog for this version.

DevC++ was definitely a hallmark in the history of open source IDE technology. Ansi and ISO C/C++ handling as-well-as the Package Manager(PackMan) threw DevC++ to the forefront.

wxDevC++ has and will continue to leverage these same powerful frameworks. The look and feel of wxDevC++ is quite similar to Visual Studio products bringing with it a familiarity. Unfortunately the controls are a bit unmanaged and thrown together at the moment.

The newer wxWidgets v2.8 recommendations for wxWidget’s namespace change is incorporated. Sizer components are however buggy and are of a serious issue within the wxWidget community. Proportions are not handled correctly at all and are paramount within the sizer struct.

In conclusion: it would appear that the product is operation with a seriously limited capacity at this point. wxDevC++ does handle generic C++, virtual classes, and references very nicely. This alone says that the product is capable.

The development stage of wxDevC++ would realistically put one in the frame of mind that: the IDE is a no holds barred peddle to the metal dev endev. Something this young with this broad a scope would have more serious issues to contend with.

the developers of wxDevC++ have been hard at work and have just started on the path. I suspect that the product with such a young and talented staff of developers is going to provide an excellent platform for cross development systems.
